Cloudseeker Mission

The Dream

In 2027, Nephelis Industries launches Cloudseeker, a $500K miniprobe, to Venus via SpaceX. Starting 07:22 PM PDT, Jul 05 2025, it explores the 50–55 km zone, collecting data on CO2, sulfuric acid, winds, and biosignatures. A 4.4 kg solar panel and 4.4 kg lithium battery power it, with a 3U CubeSat relaying real-time data to Earth. Volunteers and StellarSpark, aided by Grok, make this viable. See NASA.

The Why

Carl Sagan’s 1967 theory of life in Venus’s clouds gains support from phosphine. EnVision reveals an Earth-like past. Cloudseeker disrupts costly space norms.

The Tech

A 1m acid-resistant balloon, inspired by Venera, carries a 1kg pod with a 4K camera and sensors. A 4.4 kg solar panel and battery support 200 km/h winds, with a 4kg CubeSat for data transmission. Ready for 2026.

Commercial Vision

Cloudseeker tests Venus’s market at 50–55 km for tourism and mining CO2/sulfur. Solar energy and research hubs add profit potential. Partners are welcome.

Launch & Journey

Feasibility

Cloudseeker’s 10-kg payload, with a 1-kg balloon and 4-kg CubeSat, targets 55 km. A 4.4 kg solar panel and battery last 30 days, relaying data via NASA’s network. SpaceX’s $5,000/kg keeps costs low.

Windows

Launch windows: 2026 summer, 2027 Jan-Mar, 2028 Aug-Oct. Target: Jan 2027 with a lunar flyby.

Route

From LEO, it slingshots past the Moon to Venus, detaching pre-entry.

BUILD: JUL 03 2025 → LAUNCH: JAN 01 2027 → DATA: JUL 01 2027
