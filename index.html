The Mission: Project AETHER

Project AETHER will send Cloudseeker, a mini‑probe, into Venus’s cloud layer to validate key assumptions for future aerostat habitats—resource use, environmental conditions, and biosignatures. The mission targets 50–55 km altitude, where temperature and pressure are near Earth‑like.

Launch target: 2027. Data from Cloudseeker will inform designs for long‑lived atmospheric stations and guide follow‑on missions.

We’re raising under $500,000 to build, test, and launch Cloudseeker. For context, Rocket Lab’s privately funded Venus Life Finder was scoped in the <~$10M range for a short cloud skim; our focus is a lean, habitation‑forward pathfinder.

Envisioning Venus

Technology: Cloudseeker

Cloudseeker is built to survive Venus’s acidic clouds long enough to return high‑value measurements. Lifetime is limited by sulfuric acid exposure and power—so we optimize for “fast science” first.

  • Acid‑resistant aerostat: Fluoropolymer envelope designed for the main cloud deck’s H₂SO₄ environment, floating in the ~50–55 km habitable band.
  • Integrated sensor pod (~1 kg): HD imaging, gas analysis, and aerosol instruments to sample CO₂/N₂ mix and cloud particulates; includes rapid optical/fluorescence methods relevant to organics.
  • 3U CubeSat relay: A lightweight orbiter acts as a bent‑pipe / store‑and‑forward link; cloud opacity and geometry drive link margins.
  • Autonomy: Onboard models assist navigation and sampling prioritization in a dynamic, sheared atmosphere.

Why Venus? The Smart Bet

Venus complements Mars. At the right altitude, it offers near‑Earth pressure/temperature and abundant solar energy—albeit with acid clouds and challenging power/radiation trade‑offs.

Venus Facts

  • Gravity: 0.904 g
  • Habitable layer: ~50–55 km with Earth‑like pressure/temperature
  • Atmosphere: ~96.5% CO₂, ~3.5% N₂ (trace SO₂, etc.)
  • Solar energy: ~1.9× Earth at TOA; reduced inside clouds

History & Context

Crowd‑funding lowers the barrier to targeted, data‑driven exploration between large flagship missions.

  • Rocket Lab – Venus Life Finder (private): short cloud transect; concept budget <~$10M; first launch window mid‑2020s.
  • NASA DAVINCI (gov’t): deep atmosphere chemistry; launch NET 2029.
  • NASA VERITAS (gov’t): orbital radar mapping; 2030s after delay.
  • ESA EnVision (gov’t): orbiter studies; early 2030s.
  • Project AETHER (crowd‑funded): habitation validation pathfinder; target 2027.

Timeline

  • 2025 — Crowdfunding & Development: Lock core specs, build engineering models of the aerostat and sensor pod, and recruit contributors.
  • 2026 — Testing & Integration: Chamber and acid‑fog tests for materials, link testing with relay, and autonomy tuning.
  • 2027 — Launch & Deployment: Deliver to launch provider, deploy Cloudseeker, and begin sampling + relay ops.
  • Beyond — Habitat Build‑out: Translate measurements into scalable aerostat designs and logistics for repeat missions.
