Cloudseeker Mission

The Dream

In 2027, Nephelis Industries launches Cloudseeker, a $500,000 miniprobe, to Venus via a SpaceX rocket. Starting 07:08 PM PDT, Jul 05 2025, it explores the 50–55 km habitable zone, gathering data on CO2, sulfuric acid, winds, and biosignatures. A 3U CubeSat relays findings, marking our first step toward commercial ventures. Powered by volunteers, donations, and StellarSpark with Grok’s trajectory insights, this mission proves low-cost space exploration is viable. Learn more at NASA’s Venus portal or The Planetary Society.

The Why

“Life might have migrated into the skies,” Carl Sagan mused in 1967 about Venus’s clouds. Phosphine detections hint at microbial life, while ESA’s EnVision reveals an Earth-like past disrupted by a greenhouse catastrophe. Cloudseeker challenges billion-dollar space norms, opening Venus for profit.

The Tech

A 1-meter balloon, built with acid-resistant polytetrafluoroethylene, glides through Venus’s haze, inspired by Venera’s resilience. Its 1-kg sensor pod—featuring a 4K camera, sensors, and a nephelometer—maps the atmosphere. Solar cells power it amid 200 km/h winds, with a 4-kg CubeSat beaming data home. Rooted in Soviet Vega’s legacy, refined for 2026, Cloudseeker is lean innovation.

Commercial Vision

Cloudseeker initiates Nephelis Industries’ strategy to commercialize Venus. The 50–55 km zone, with Earth-like pressure and temperatures, supports tourism—imagine cloud city adventures. Mining can extract CO2 for industrial use or sulfur for compounds, despite metal scarcity. Beyond these, energy from solar arrays or research hubs for exoplanet studies promise profit. This mission tests the market, inviting global partners.

Launch & Journey

Cloudseeker’s journey is a strategic leap, testing Venus’s commercial potential. Track our progress and join the mission.

Technical Feasibility

Cloudseeker’s 10-kg payload—a 1-kg balloon probe and 4-kg CubeSat—targets the 55-km cloud layer, mimicking Earth’s pressure and temperature. Its acid-resistant shield, solar cells, and radio sustain 30 days. The CubeSat relays data via NASA’s Deep Space Network. SpaceX’s $5,000/kg rideshare keeps costs under $50,000, driven by volunteers.

Launch Windows (2025–2030)

Venus windows open every 19 months: summer 2026, January–March 2027, August–October 2028. We aim for January 2027, with a lunar flyby and 5–6-month cruise.

Route to Venus

From LEO, Cloudseeker’s Photon bus slingshots past the Moon, arcing to Venus. The probe detaches 30 minutes before entry, floating at 55 km.
