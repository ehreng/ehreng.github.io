Mission Overview

The Dream: Launched in 2027, Cloudseeker by Nephelis Industries targets Venus’s 50–55 km zone. Starting 07:40 PM PDT, Jul 05 2025, it uses a 4.4 kg solar panel and 4.4 kg lithium battery to gather real-time data on CO2, sulfuric acid, winds, and biosignatures. A 3U CubeSat relays findings, proving affordable exploration with volunteers and Grok’s AI. Explore more at NASA.

The Why: Venus’s clouds whisper of life—Sagan’s 1967 dream, fueled by phosphine and EnVision’s Earth-like past. Cloudseeker turns this into profit.

The Tech: A 1m acid-resistant balloon carries a 1kg pod with a 4K camera and sensors. Solar power and a battery tackle 200 km/h winds, with a 4kg CubeSat beaming data home. Ready by 2026.

Why Venus?

Venus’s 50–55 km clouds offer Earth-like temps and pressure, shielding futuristic habitats. Phosphine hints at life, while HAVOC inspires solar cities. Cloudseeker unlocks this sci-fi frontier for tourism and mining.
